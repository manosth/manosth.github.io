Manos Theodosis

PhD candidate, Harvard University
etheodosis@g.harvard.edu
     
I am currently a G1 at Harvard University, pursuing a PhD in Computer Science. My research advisor is Demba Ba and I am a member of the CRISP lab. I finished my undergraduate studies at the National Technical University of Athens in Greece. My thesis was conducted at the CVSP lab under the supervision of Petros Maragos. My thesis focused on using tropical geometry and vector space theory in order to model and analyse Weighted Finite State Transducers' algorithms. It is available here (in Greek).

My research interests include algebraic geometry, sparse representations, statistical machine learning, and machine learning theory.

Overall I am interested in the interpretation of problems, algorithms, and structures through mathematics. To that end, I find particular areas of mathematics extremely intriguing:

  • I am interested in geometry in all forms; from fractal theory, to tropical geometry, to algebraic geometry. Regarding the first two, I have extensive experience through a graduate course I took, and my thesis.
  • I am interested in vector space theory. Different bases can offer different tradeoffs for modern problems and can very efficiently describe problems.
  • I am interested in spectral theory. It can reason about the properties of a structure without explicitly studying it.
  • I am intrigued by machine learning as I think it aims to solve many interesting problems. Thus, I am interested in exploring how the above areas of mathematics can be applied to machine learning in order to solve its problems more efficiently, guided by intuition.
