I finished my undergraduate studies at the National Technical University of Athens in Greece, under the supervision of Petros Maragos. My thesis focused on using tropical geometry and vector space theory in order to model and analyse Weighted Finite State Transducers' algorithms. It is available here (in Greek).

My current research focuses on the extension of the results of my thesis in two ways:
  • use vector space theory to analyse the solution space of the algorithms. A possible application is the examination of the effect different space bases have on the WFST structure.
  • analyze the spectral properties of the matrices in WFSTs. In much of the modeling, eigenvector matrices naturally occured, and they could be interpreted.
I am also working on some other ideas:
  • using tropical lines to specify shapes in the Euclidean space using fewer parameters.
  • extending the previous idea to introduce a new representation format for images, leading to higher compression rates.
Currently, I am working at the CVSP lab of NTUA as a Research Intern on a project. We are trying to use smart watches to monitor patients with mental illness and detect possible remissions.

Overall I am interested in the interpretation of problems, algorithms, and structures through mathematics. To that end, I find particular areas of mathematics extremely intriguing:

  • I am interested in geometry in all forms; from fractal theory, to tropical geometry, to algebraic geometry. Regarding the first two, I have extensive experience through a graduate course I took, and my thesis.
  • I am interested in vector space theory. Different bases can offer different tradeoffs for modern problems and can very efficiently describe problems.
  • I am interested in spectral theory. It can reason about the properties of a structure without explicitly studying it.
  • I am intrigued by machine learning as I think it aims to solve many interesting problems. Thus, I am interested in exploring how the above areas of mathematics can be applied to machine learning in order to solve its problems more efficiently, guided by intuition.

My work during my thesis was very fruitful. Interesting ideas lead to a few publications on international conferences:

  • [1] Theodosis E., Maragos P. "Analysis of the viterbi algorithm using tropical algebra and geometry", International Workshop on Signal Processing Advances in Wireless Communications, 2018
  • [2] (Under review) Theodosis E., Maragos P. "Tropical modeling of weighted transducers algorithms on graphs", International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing, 2019
  • [3] (Under review) Theodosis E., Maragos P. "An adaptive pruning algorithm for spoofing localisation based on tropical geometry", International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing, 2019
